Vacation

Seven days in the Lion City — gardens that glow, hawker stalls that humble you, and a skyline so audacious it feels like the future arrived early.

Sanjay Dhiman • April 29, 2026 • 9 min read

Singapore was never on my bucket list the way Bali or Tokyo was. It felt too polished, too planned — a city-state that ran like a Swiss watch and left little room for the delightful chaos I usually chase when I travel. I was wrong. Completely, embarrassingly, wonderfully wrong. Seven days in Singapore changed how I think about what a city can be, what food can mean, and how a tiny island the size of a large Indian district can hold enough experiences to fill a lifetime of stories.

This is that story — day by day, bite by bite, skyline by skyline.

✈️ Getting there: Direct flights from Bengaluru (Kempegowda International) to Changi Airport run daily with Singapore Airlines and IndiGo. Flight time is roughly 4.5 hours. Changi itself is worth arriving early for — it consistently ranks as the world's best airport, and the Jewel waterfall alone is worth the terminal walk.

Day by Day — The Full Itinerary

🛬 Arrival & Marina Bay — Getting Your Bearings

Land at Changi, spend an hour wandering the Jewel — the indoor waterfall cascading through a glass dome is genuinely jaw-dropping and a perfect first impression. Take the MRT to your hotel. Check in, freshen up, then head to the Marina Bay Sands skypark at golden hour. The 57th-floor observation deck gives you the full Singapore panorama — the financial district gleaming, the Gardens by the Bay stretching below, the strait connecting you to Indonesia.

  • 🌊 Jewel Changi waterfall (free to walk through)
  • 🏨 Check in — Bugis or Clarke Quay areas are great bases
  • 🌆 MBS SkyPark observation deck at sunset (~SGD 32)
  • 🍜 Dinner at Lau Pa Sat hawker centre — try the satay stalls

🌿 Gardens by the Bay & Chinatown

Start early at Gardens by the Bay before the heat kicks in. The Supertree Grove is best experienced at opening time when the crowds are thin. Walk the OCBC Skyway between the trees for views back toward the skyline. Then hop a cab to Chinatown — the Sri Mariamman Temple tucked among shophouses is a vivid cultural contrast, and the Maxwell Food Centre nearby serves some of the best chicken rice in the world.

  • 🌳 Supertree Grove & Cloud Forest dome
  • 🌉 OCBC Skyway aerial walkway (~SGD 14)
  • 🛕 Sri Mariamman Temple — Singapore's oldest Hindu temple
  • 🍚 Maxwell Food Centre: Tian Tian Chicken Rice (queue worth it)
  • 🌙 Return for Supertree light show at 7:45 PM (free)

🦁 Sentosa Island — Beaches & Universal Studios

Dedicate a full day to Sentosa. Take the Sentosa Express from VivoCity. If you are an amusement park person, Universal Studios Singapore is genuinely excellent — the Battlestar Galactica duelling coasters alone are worth it. If you prefer to chill, Siloso Beach has good vibes and cheap cocktails. Watch the Wings of Time light and water show in the evening — it is cheesy in the best possible way.

  • 🎢 Universal Studios Singapore (book ahead, ~SGD 83)
  • 🏖️ Siloso or Palawan Beach for afternoon downtime
  • 🌊 Wings of Time show at sunset (~SGD 18)
  • 🍹 Dinner at VivoCity — good seafood options on the rooftop

🕌 Little India, Arab Street & the Colonial Core

This day is pure neighbourhood culture. Start in Little India — the colours, smells, and sounds of Serangoon Road will feel oddly familiar if you are Indian, yet entirely distinct. The Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple is spectacular. Walk to Kampong Glam and the Sultan Mosque, then browse the fabric shops on Arab Street. End the afternoon at the Raffles Hotel for a Singapore Sling — touristy, yes, but the Long Bar is a piece of history and the drink was invented here.

  • 🌺 Serangoon Road flower stalls & Mustafa Centre
  • 🕌 Sultan Mosque & Haji Lane boutique shopping
  • 🏛️ Raffles Hotel Long Bar — Singapore Sling (~SGD 37)
  • 🍛 Dinner at Komala Vilas — legendary South Indian vegetarian

🌏 Singapore Zoo & Night Safari

Singapore's zoo is consistently rated among the best in the world — and once you visit, you understand why. The open-concept enclosures feel nothing like a traditional zoo. Spend the morning here, have lunch on-site, and then stay for the Night Safari in the evening. Tram rides through the nocturnal animal habitats are genuinely magical — white rhinos, fishing cats, and clouded leopards lit by nothing but carefully placed amber light.

  • 🦏 Singapore Zoo morning visit (~SGD 49)
  • 🌙 Night Safari tram experience (~SGD 55)
  • 🍽️ Bongo Burgers inside the zoo — surprisingly good
  • 🐆 Creatures of the Night show at 7:30 PM (included)

🏙️ Orchard Road, Clarke Quay & Rooftop Bars

Day six is indulgence and nightlife. Spend the morning on Orchard Road — Singapore's luxury shopping boulevard — even if you are not buying anything, people-watching here is exceptional. Afternoon: head to Haw Par Villa if you are curious about something genuinely surreal (giant statues depicting Chinese mythology and hell). Evening: Clarke Quay comes alive after dark — rooftop bars like 1-Altitude give you the full city-at-night experience.

  • 🛍️ Orchard Road window shopping & ION mall food court
  • 🎭 Haw Par Villa — free, bizarre, unforgettable
  • 🌃 Clarke Quay riverside bars from 7 PM
  • 🍸 1-Altitude rooftop bar — best city night view

☀️ Last Morning, Hawker Hunt & Departure

Save the last morning for one final hawker breakfast — head to Old Airport Road Food Centre, one of the oldest and most authentic hawker centres in the city. Order chee cheong fun, kaya toast with soft-boiled eggs and kopi, and maybe a bowl of laksa for good measure. Do your last souvenir shopping at Bugis Street market, then head to Changi for your flight. Give yourself extra time — you will want to wander the airport one more time.

  • 🍳 Kaya toast & kopi breakfast — Ya Kun or Toast Box
  • 🍜 Old Airport Road Food Centre — laksa, chee cheong fun
  • 🛍️ Bugis Street for last-minute souvenirs
  • ✈️ Changi Airport — early arrival recommended, enjoy the Jewel again

Practical Tips for Indian Travellers

💳

Money & Budget

Singapore is expensive but manageable. Hawker meals cost SGD 4–8. Budget SGD 150–200/day total including accommodation if you plan well.

🚇

Getting Around

The MRT is exceptional — fast, clean, and covers everything. Get an EZ-Link card at the airport. Grab (ride-hailing) fills the gaps.

🌡️

Weather

Hot and humid year-round (~30°C). Carry a compact umbrella — afternoon showers are frequent. Light cotton clothes only.

🍽️

Food Strategy

Eat at hawker centres for authentic, cheap, incredible food. Skip hotel restaurants. Vegetarian options are plentiful — just ask.

📱

SIM Card

Pick up a Singtel or StarHub tourist SIM at Changi — 7-day unlimited data plans run around SGD 15–20. Google Maps works flawlessly.

🪪

Visa for Indians

Indian passport holders need a visa. Apply online through the ICA website. Approval is typically quick (1–3 working days) for tourism.

What Nobody Tells You About Singapore

The thing that surprised me most was not the cleanliness or the efficiency — I had expected both. It was the depth. Beneath the glass towers and manicured gardens is a genuinely multicultural city that has somehow found a way to let Malay, Chinese, Indian, and colonial heritage coexist not just peacefully but beautifully. You hear four official languages in a single taxi ride. You eat Tamil food for breakfast, Hokkien mee for lunch, and Malay satay for dinner, and none of it feels forced or touristic — it just is what Singapore is.

The hawker culture hit me hardest. I had eaten at Michelin-starred restaurants and street-food stalls across Asia, but a SGD 5 plate of char kway teow from a 70-year-old wok master at Newton Food Centre stopped me mid-bite. That is real cooking — generational knowledge in a single dish, cooked for you in under three minutes at a folding table under a corrugated roof. Singapore has the good sense to protect this as national heritage.

"Singapore makes you reconsider what a city can aspire to — not just as infrastructure, but as a lived experience."

Would I Go Back?

Without hesitation. Seven days felt both plenty and not nearly enough. I left with a list of hawker stalls I did not get to, neighbourhoods I only glimpsed from a cab window, and a promise to myself to do the cable car over to Mount Faber next time. There is always a next time with Singapore — the city earns it.

If you are weighing your next international trip and Singapore is on the shortlist, move it to the top. It is the most livable, most visitable, most surprising place I have been in years. The Lion City bites — in the best possible way.

🦁 Final tip: Book the Gardens by the Bay Cloud Forest and Flower Dome tickets online in advance — they sell out, especially on weekends. The Cloud Forest dome is a genuinely otherworldly experience: a 35-metre indoor waterfall surrounded by tropical plants from misty mountain climates. Do not skip it.
